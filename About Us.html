About Us



Our Story

Incorporated in 2000, Stawi is a Kenyan Non-government Organization with its headquaters in Nairobi county. Stawi started its operations for the first time in 2015. Stawi is an idea conceived out of the need to ensure sustainable farming, and addressing the diverse farming needs of Kenyan farmers. Therefore, the organization advocates for a competitive, informed and susstainable agricultural practices. Zero hunger remains one of the major sustainable development goals, and this can be achieved through working closely and investing in both the large scale and small scale farmers in the country. With Stawi, farmers are not only able to produce enough, but can diversify their farming and increase their household income through selling surplus produce. as a master thesis recommendation to advocate fo incomes through selling the surplus products.

Who We Are

Stawi is a registered NGO in Kenya under the NGO Act. We deal with smallholder farmers. We train them and offer them with up to date information and innovative technologies to enable them produce more and earn a better living from farming. We work hand in hand with smallholder women farmers who are the majority farmers in the region.

What we offer

  • We provide training to smallholder farmers on modern farming practices to enable them produce more with little resources available to them.
  • We provide an interactive mobile phone supported platform where farmers can access and share agricultural related information and learn from other farmers.
  • We train our farmers on where to get information about seeedlings, especially the Hass and Fuerte seedlings
